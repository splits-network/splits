# Glossary, Terminology, and Data Model Reference

## Core Terminology

### Platform Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Employment Networks** | Parent company and brand operating Splits Network and Applicant Network |
| **Splits Network** | Recruiter and company-facing platform for collaborative split-fee recruiting (splits.network) |
| **Applicant Network** | Candidate-facing portal for job search and application tracking (applicant.network) |
| **Split-Fee Placement** | A recruitment arrangement where multiple recruiters collaborate on filling a position, splitting the placement fee based on their respective contributions |
| **Split** | The percentage or dollar amount of a placement fee attributed to a specific role in the placement |

### User Types

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Recruiter** | A recruiting professional who sources candidates, submits them to jobs, and earns commission splits |
| **Company** | A hiring organization that posts job openings and reviews candidate submissions |
| **Candidate** | A job seeker who applies for positions or is submitted by a recruiter |
| **Firm** | A recruiting agency (team of recruiters) operating on the platform with shared resources and admin controls |
| **Company Admin** | A company user with full access to manage jobs, review candidates, and handle billing |
| **Hiring Manager** | A company user with scoped access to review candidates within assigned departments/roles |
| **Platform Admin** | An Employment Networks staff member with full platform access |

### Commission Roles

| Term | Nickname | Definition |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Candidate Recruiter** | Closer | The recruiter who represents and submits the candidate to a specific job |
| **Company Recruiter** | Client / Hiring Facilitator | The recruiter who represents the company's interests in the hiring process |
| **Job Owner** | Specs Owner | The recruiter who originally created the job posting on behalf of a company |
| **Candidate Sourcer** | Discovery | The recruiter who first brought the candidate to the platform (attributed via referral codes) |
| **Company Sourcer** | BD (Business Development) | The recruiter who first brought the company to the platform |

### ATS Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Application** | A candidate's submission to a specific job, tracked through a multi-stage pipeline |
| **Placement** | The record created when a candidate is successfully hired, tracking salary, fees, and commission attribution |
| **Pipeline** | The sequence of stages an application moves through, from draft to hired |
| **Gate** | An approval checkpoint in the pipeline where a specific party must review and approve before proceeding |
| **Pre-Screen** | Custom screening questions attached to a job that candidates must answer |
| **Application Note** | A threaded message or record attached to an application, with typed categories and visibility controls |
| **Pitch** | A recruiter's written advocacy for why a candidate is a strong fit for a role |

### Financial Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Placement Fee** | The total fee charged to a company upon a successful hire, typically a percentage of first-year salary |
| **Commission Split** | The division of the placement fee among the attributed roles |
| **Splits Rate** | The percentage allocated to each commission role, varies by plan tier |
| **Escrow Hold** | A temporary hold on payout funds during the guarantee period |
| **Guarantee Period** | The time window (default 90 days) during which a company can request a replacement if the hire doesn't work out |
| **Placement Snapshot** | An immutable record freezing the commission attribution and rates at the moment of hire |
| **Admin Take Rate** | The percentage a firm (recruiting agency) takes from member recruiters' payouts |
| **Net Terms** | The payment timeline for company invoices (immediate, net-30, net-60, net-90) |

### Platform Features

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **AI Fit Analysis** | Automated evaluation of candidate-job compatibility providing fit scores, strengths, concerns, and skill gap analysis |
| **Match Score** | A composite score combining rule-based, skills-based, and AI-powered similarity scoring |
| **True Match** | A high-confidence match between a candidate and job, exceeding standard match thresholds |
| **Referral Code** | A unique code generated by a recruiter that attributes permanent sourcer credit for any candidate or company who signs up using it |
| **CRA (Candidate Role Assignment)** | The deal record tying a candidate to a job with explicit recruiter roles assigned — the wiring harness for gate routing and commission attribution |
| **Identity Masking** | The practice of hiding a candidate's full identity from companies until formal acceptance, showing only initials and masked email |

### Gamification Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **XP (Experience Points)** | Points earned from platform activities that contribute to level progression |
| **Level** | A progression tier based on total XP, with associated titles and perks |
| **Badge** | An achievement award with tiered levels (bronze, silver, gold, platinum) |
| **Streak** | A measure of consecutive activity on the platform |
| **Leaderboard** | Rankings of users by XP for different time periods and entity types |

## Application Pipeline Stages

The complete set of stages an application can pass through:

| Stage | Description | Who Acts |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| `draft` | Candidate is working on the application | Candidate |
| `ai_review` | AI is analyzing candidate-job fit | System |
| `ai_reviewed` | AI review complete, awaiting candidate action | Candidate |
| `recruiter_request` | Recruiter requested more information | Recruiter |
| `recruiter_proposed` | Recruiter proposed this job to the candidate | Recruiter |
| `recruiter_review` | Recruiter reviewing before submission | Recruiter |
| `screen` | Initial screening phase | Recruiter |
| `submitted` | Submitted to company | System |
| `company_review` | Company reviewing candidate | Company |
| `company_feedback` | Company provided feedback, awaiting next action | Company |
| `interview` | Interview scheduled or in progress | Company + Candidate |
| `offer` | Offer extended to candidate | Company |
| `hired` | Candidate accepted offer (terminal) | Candidate |
| `rejected` | Application rejected (terminal) | Company or Recruiter |
| `withdrawn` | Candidate withdrew application (terminal) | Candidate |
| `expired` | Application timed out without action (terminal) | System |

## Relationship Types and Statuses

### Recruiter-Company Relationship
- **Statuses:** Pending → Active → (optionally) Declined or Terminated
- **Types:** Sourcer or Recruiter
- **Permissions:** `can_manage_company_jobs` flag
- Either party can terminate with a reason

### Recruiter-Candidate Relationship
- **Invitation workflow:** Token-based with 7-day expiry
- **Statuses:** Pending → Active → (optionally) Terminated
- Termination cascades to affected applications

### Collaboration (Recruiter-to-Recruiter)
- **Proposal workflow:** One recruiter proposes collaboration with clear split terms
- **Statuses:** Invited → Accepted or Declined

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General

**Q: What is the relationship between Splits Network and Applicant Network?**
A: Splits Network is the platform for recruiters and companies — where roles are posted, split-fee partnerships are managed, and placements happen. Applicant Network is the candidate-facing platform where job seekers apply, get matched with recruiters, and track their applications. Both platforms share the same backend and database, creating a seamless ecosystem.

**Q: Is Applicant Network really free?**
A: Absolutely. Candidates will never pay a dime. There are no premium tiers, no upsells, and no hidden costs.

**Q: How is this different from LinkedIn or Indeed?**
A: Instead of blasting applications into the void, candidates get matched with real recruiters who specialize in their field. Recruiters advocate for candidates, prep them for interviews, and ensure they never get ghosted. The platform measures recruiter responsiveness, creating accountability.

**Q: Will candidates actually hear back?**
A: Recruiters on the platform are measured by responsiveness. The platform maintains a 95% response rate within 48 hours.

### For Recruiters

**Q: How do I earn money on Splits Network?**
A: You earn a percentage of the placement fee for each role you fill. The exact percentage depends on which commission roles you hold (Closer, Client, Specs Owner, Discovery, BD) and your subscription plan tier. You can hold multiple roles on a single placement.

**Q: What if a placement doesn't work out?**
A: Each placement has a guarantee period (default 90 days). If the hire leaves during this period, the company can request a replacement and payouts are adjusted accordingly. Escrow holds protect all parties during this period.

**Q: Can I work with other recruiters?**
A: Yes — collaboration is a core feature. You can propose partnerships on specific placements with clear split terms, or join a recruiting firm to collaborate with a team.

### For Companies

**Q: How much does it cost to post a job?**
A: Free. There are no listing fees. You only pay when a hire is made — a placement fee as a percentage of the candidate's first-year salary, which you set.

**Q: How is the fee structured?**
A: You set the total fee percentage. The platform's 5-role commission system automatically distributes the fee among the recruiters who contributed to the placement, plus the platform's share.

**Q: Can we use our existing ATS alongside Splits Network?**
A: Yes. Splits Network integrates bidirectionally with Greenhouse, Lever, Workable, and Ashby, allowing you to sync roles, candidates, and status updates between systems.
