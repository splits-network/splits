# Role-Specific Workflows and Functional Details

## For Recruiters — The Splits Network Experience

### Getting Started

1. **Sign Up & Get Verified** — Create an account on splits.network, complete your profile with specialties, industries, and experience
2. **Choose Your Role** — Operate as an independent recruiter or join a recruiting firm (agency) on the platform
3. **Select a Plan** — Start free (Starter), upgrade to Pro ($99/mo) or Partner ($249/mo) for enhanced payout bonuses
4. **Browse Available Roles** — Access a curated marketplace of open positions from verified companies

### Daily Workflow

1. **Discover Roles** — Browse and search open positions filtered by industry, location, employment type, job level, salary range, and commute type
2. **Source Candidates** — Add candidates to your pipeline (recruiter-managed) or invite existing candidates to self-manage their profiles
3. **Submit Candidates** — Submit qualified candidates to roles through the ATS pipeline with supporting notes and pitch
4. **AI Review Loop** — Applications go through AI fit analysis; candidates can revise based on AI feedback before final submission
5. **Gate Approvals** — Navigate multi-gate approval process (candidate recruiter → company recruiter → company decision)
6. **Collaborate** — Propose collaboration with other recruiters on placements, with clear split terms
7. **Track Placements** — Monitor active placements through guarantee periods
8. **Get Paid** — Automated commission payouts via Stripe after guarantee completion

### Commission Roles a Recruiter Can Hold

| Role | Nickname | How You Earn It |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Candidate Recruiter | Closer | You represent and submit the candidate |
| Company Recruiter | Client / Hiring Facilitator | You represent the company's interests |
| Job Owner | Specs Owner | You created the job posting on behalf of a company |
| Candidate Sourcer | Discovery | You first brought the candidate to the platform (via referral code) |
| Company Sourcer | BD (Business Development) | You first brought the company to the platform |

A single recruiter can hold multiple roles on a single placement, maximizing their earning potential. Unfilled roles' commission percentage goes to the platform.

### Recruiter Tools

- **Candidate Management** — Full ATS pipeline with stage tracking, notes, and document management
- **Recruiter-Candidate Relationships** — Formal connection with invitation workflow (7-day token expiry)
- **Recruiter-Company Relationships** — Connect with companies, manage permissions, access their roles
- **Referral Codes** — Generate branded referral codes that earn permanent sourcer attribution
- **Marketplace Profile** — Public profile with industries, specialties, skills, placement history
- **Firm Membership** — Join or create recruiting agencies with shared candidate pools and admin take rates
- **AI Matching** — Get matched with candidates based on rule-based + AI-powered scoring
- **Real-Time Chat** — Message candidates, collaborators, and company contacts directly
- **Gamification** — Earn XP, badges, and leaderboard rankings for active engagement

## For Companies — The Splits Network Experience

### Getting Started

1. **Create Your Company** — Register your organization on splits.network
2. **Post Roles for Free** — Create detailed job listings with requirements, salary ranges, and fee structures
3. **Set Your Terms** — Define fee percentages, guarantee periods (default 90 days), and split structures
4. **Access the Network** — Instantly connect with 2,000+ specialized recruiters

### Daily Workflow

1. **Post Roles** — Create jobs with rich details: title, descriptions (separate recruiter and candidate versions), requirements (mandatory vs. preferred), responsibilities, salary range, employment type, commute type, job level, company perks, and culture tags
2. **Review Submissions** — Evaluate recruiter-submitted candidates through the multi-gate approval process
3. **Manage Pipeline** — Track all candidates across stages: submitted → company review → interview → offer → hired
4. **Provide Feedback** — Use application notes to communicate with recruiters (with visibility controls: shared, company-only, or candidate-only)
5. **Make Hires** — Convert successful candidates to placements with automatic commission calculation
6. **Track Financials** — Invoices, billing profiles with net terms (immediate, net-30, net-60, net-90), and dispute resolution

### Company-Specific Features

- **Candidate Identity Masking** — Candidates' full identity is hidden until formal acceptance, preventing bypass of the platform
- **Pre-Screening Questions** — Define custom screening questions per job (text, yes/no, select, multi-select)
- **Multiple User Roles** — Company Admin (full access) and Hiring Manager (scoped review access)
- **Recruiter Network Management** — Connect with specific recruiters, control who can manage your jobs
- **Pipeline Visibility** — See exactly where every candidate stands across all your open roles
- **Billing Flexibility** — Net terms, Stripe invoicing, fee dispute resolution
- **Company Reputation** — Track and build your reputation score on the platform

### Fee Structure

- **Free to post roles** — no listing fees
- **Pay only on successful hire** — placement fee as a percentage of candidate's first-year salary
- **Company sets the fee percentage** — full control over terms
- **Guarantee period** — default 90 days; if the hire doesn't work out, no fee
- **Transparent split visibility** — see exactly how fees are distributed

## Cross-Role Interactions

### How the Three Roles Connect

| Interaction | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Recruiter → Company** | Recruiters connect with companies via relationship requests; companies approve and set permissions |
| **Recruiter → Candidate** | Recruiters invite candidates via tokens (7-day expiry); candidates accept to establish formal relationship |
| **Candidate → Company** | Candidates apply to company roles; identity is masked until company accepts the submission |
| **Recruiter → Recruiter** | Collaboration proposals for co-working placements with explicit split terms |
| **Company → Recruiter** | Companies can invite specific recruiters to work their roles |

### Relationship Lifecycle

All relationships (recruiter-company, recruiter-candidate) follow a lifecycle:
- `pending` → `active` → optionally `terminated`
- Either party can terminate with a stated reason
- Termination triggers cascading decisions on affected open jobs and active applications
- The platform provides "affected-by-termination" views to help manage the transition
